Roborovski Dwarf Hamster

The smallest hamster species in the Phodopus genus is the Roborovski hamster, or Robo for short. These tiny rodents can be cautious, but they are usually fairly swift and inquisitive.

      The Robo hamster, also referred to as the Robo hamster for short, is the tiniest species of hamster in the Phodopus genus. These little rodents, sometimes known as the Robo dwarf hamster, are typically highly swift and curious, yet they can also be cautious. The majority of hamster species get along with one another and are calm, peaceful animals. They can also gain confidence around individuals.

      Due to their small size, agility, and cautious attitude, they aren't the simplest to hand-tame. They have white dots above their eyes that resemble eyebrows and are typically sandy in color, however there are many different varieties. Robo hamsters are easy to care for; they just need a simple feed and routine habitat cleaning.

      The length and weight of a roborovski hamster are each roughly two inches. They are less than an inch length at birth. They mature at about two months of age.

      As nocturnal animals, robo hamsters often awaken at sunset and remain busy all night. While they occasionally make very quiet vocalizations, for some persons the noise they make at night may be an issue. You really shouldn't keep their enclosure in your bedroom if you have trouble falling asleep.

      These hamsters can be amusing to watch and are relatively low-maintenance pets. However, they are not really cuddly creatures and often dislike being handled a much. Although they are often calm, if you surprise them they may bite.
